## Key Information


- Jeff Denworth stated he is rapidly losing confidence in VAST closing a Q1 deal with Microsoft AI Infrastructure (MAI) and believes VAST's lack of aggressive execution is a key contributor.

- Microsoft AI Infrastructure (MAI) needs 2 exabytes (2EB) of storage capacity deployed 'on the floor' in 2025, per Jeff Denworth's summary of the situation.

- Jeff Denworth reported global supply chain turmoil and that VAST likely cannot support the MAI scale natively via Arrow or Avnet supply chain channels.

- Jeff Denworth reported Microsoft is also in a difficult supply chain position but has a large allocation across Azure.

- Jeff Denworth reported Microsoft is considering HDDs for the first April delivery for MAI scale-up, and that HDD supply is also constrained.

- Jeff Denworth stated that testing at CoreWeave for this MAI-related effort has not started in earnest and that VAST has not started commercial discussions for the deal.

- Jeff Denworth asserted Microsoft must order hardware early to utilize GPUs quickly, and therefore must decide on storage software early because software choice materially impacts the hardware purchase.

- Jeff Denworth stated VAST is the most infrastructure-efficient option for Microsoft MAI at scale and pushed the team to act accordingly.

- Jonsi Stefansson reported he met with Microsoft contacts named Aung and Maneesh, and they expect VAST to provide an integration proposal with Azure Blob.

- Jonsi Stefansson reported he emailed multiple Microsoft contacts to understand Microsoft supply chain status and platform needs, but received out-of-office replies indicating they return on 2026-01-05.

- Jeff Denworth referenced a prior CoreWeave deal where VAST had Andy and Leo working daily from CoreWeave offices, which Jeff credited with generating $1.5B in bookings so far.
