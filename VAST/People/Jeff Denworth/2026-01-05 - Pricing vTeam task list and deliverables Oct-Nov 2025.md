## Key Information


- VAST Data cloud pricing work included defining a cloud private-offer pricing structure and discount guidance focused on capacity-only pricing for immediate use by the field.

- VAST Data planned a public PAYGO offer for a customer-tenant deployment model, including consumption metering and billing, with an initial target of approximately 6 months from the planning point in late 2025.

- VAST Data planned a full multi-tenant SaaS offering using VAST's own account model, with target timing described as likely FY28, and required alignment of pricing and COGS model.

- The Pricing vTeam defined and published a cloud-specific discount policy for private offers to avoid extreme discounts.

- The Pricing vTeam evaluated whether to introduce a fixed cores-per-PB normalization model for cloud pricing starting 2026-02-01 and documented the model decision.

- VAST Data defined marketplace entitlements and a pricing schema for a Google private offer and began the approval process.

- VAST Data performed competitive cloud pricing analysis against Pure, NetApp, Isilon, WEKA, and Hammerspace, including pricing models and price points.

- VAST Data evaluated using performance tiers, such as instance class or throughput per PB, to simplify cloud pricing communication.

- VAST Data defined a hybrid ELA conversion approach and deployment tracking across on-prem and cloud in Polaris and Uplink.

- VAST Data evaluated feasibility and policy for an optional 'unlock all cores' add-on price in cloud, meaning no core disabling.

- Tomer Hagay investigated Azure Compute Unit and other cross-generation normalization metrics for potential future use in cloud pricing normalization.

- VAST Data worked with Tackle on IO for overages and pricing EULA needs related to cloud pricing operations.

- Jason Vallery drafted a unit-based pricing proposal referencing Databricks and Snowflake, including ARR impact modeling and discount guardrails.

- Timo Pervane shared an investor valuation framework and metrics, including how ARR is defined and measured and implications for Cloud Solutions.

- Timo Pervane defined discounting policy and controls to prevent deal-level leakage when cloud SKUs are undiscountable.

- Timo Pervane progressed hiring of a Finance Business Partner for Cloud Solutions and planned to embed the role with the Cloud Solutions leadership team.

- Timo Pervane provided pricing and discounting analysis using price-paid scatter by cohort to inform the cloud normalization plan and sent the dataset with initial insights.
