## Decisions


- Defer drafting and socializing a product management discipline proposal until after the Tel Aviv onsite the following week, to gather context and align in person.

- Any product management operating model changes (OKRs, traceability, cadence) should be applied across the full product stack, not only the cloud area, because cloud delivery depends on core product engineering.




## Key Information


- Jason Vallery observed that product management at VAST Data is run very differently than at Microsoft, and he is still determining how much to insert himself into changing PM practices.

- Jason Vallery described a Microsoft-style PM operating model: durable objectives, measurable key results, monthly rhythm of business reviews, and hierarchical traceability from dev tasks to features to epics/user stories to KRs to objectives.

- Tomer Hagay said he had a vision for more structured PM at VAST Data (hierarchy of tasks and goals with measurable outcomes) but believes the organization is not currently capable of operating in that mode without leadership prioritization.

- Tomer Hagay stated that Renan is resistant to traditional product management authority and does not want sales to say they will prioritize with product management, asserting that customers are the product management function.

- Tomer Hagay reported that VAST Data lacks structured visibility into feature delivery status (epics and tickets), forcing manual status gathering by contacting engineers, which slows responses to field and customer questions.

- Jason Vallery asserted that implementing PM rigor requires top-down alignment and a two-in-a-box model with engineering leadership and product strategy leadership aligned, and cannot be driven bottom-up by Tomer Hagay alone.
