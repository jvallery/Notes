## Key Information

- Liraz Ben Or has worked at VAST Data for almost six years (six years in March 2026).

- Liraz Ben Or is Head of Program Management at VAST Data and leads major release program management with a team of three.

- Liraz Ben Or is deeply involved in release management and is responsible for VAST Data release phase gates (she invites stakeholders to phase gate meetings).

- VAST Data major releases use a waterfall-style process with four phase gates: (1) scope/content review, (2) plan plus change management to adjust commitments, (3) code freeze and beta readiness, and (4) release.

- VAST Data QA runs three cycles per release: Phase 1 runs all tests, Phase 2 re-runs failed tests, and a Golden Run re-runs all tests again; target QA success rate is approximately 96-97%.

- VAST Data release cadence is roughly every five months, with overlapping work where some developers shift to planning the next release during the current release code freeze.

- VAST Data maintains multiple release trains concurrently: 5.4 was released the week prior to 2025-10-29; 5.5 is in progress; 5.6 planning is starting; 5.3 and 5.2 are still maintained; 5.1 is hotfix-only.

- VAST Data is releasing additional 5.2 minors (e.g., 5.2.4) due to field upgrade issues requiring fixes, despite not planning to continue 5.2 minors originally.

- VAST Data has customers still running 5.1 in the field; 5.1 receives only hotfixes and no regular minor releases.

- VAST Data aims for a 'major minus two' support posture, but customers often remain on older versions because upgrades are difficult; the organization provides hotfixes when needed.

- VAST Data's R&D organization split includes core development for future major releases and a separate vForce group responsible for field releases and escalations; developers may rotate between groups, and vForce work is high-stress with after-hours escalation expectations.

- VAST Data prioritization is strongly field and customer driven; product curates a customer-weighted feature list for upcoming releases, and change management is used to drop or stretch features when commitments cannot be met.

- VAST Data release artifacts include per-release wiki pages (status, owners, dates), Gantt plans (often in Excel), QA tracking in QTest, and weekly internal status decks.

- VAST Data pushed expedited milestones in release 5.4 due to Tesla requirements.

- Liraz Ben Or stated that 5.4 was not yet stable enough at the time of the meeting and the organization was primarily pushing customers to 5.3 until 5.4 stabilizes.
